# Meals-on-Wheels – Smart Food Access Platform

# Overview
Meals-on-Wheels is a web-based prototype designed to improve food accessibility in underserved communities by connecting residents with nearby food distribution points and meal providers.

The platform visualizes food access points on an interactive map, allowing users to easily locate nearby meal distribution centers, community kitchens, and support services.

The project aims to address food deserts, where communities lack convenient access to affordable and nutritious food. These challenges often affect low-income communities, seniors, and individuals with mobility limitations. Programs like Meals on Wheels deliver nutritious meals and support services to vulnerable populations, helping them maintain independence and improve nutrition.

This project was developed as part of the Reboot the Earth Hackathon, focusing on innovative solutions for improving community food access.

# Problem Statement: 

Many urban communities face limited access to fresh and nutritious food due to:

Lack of nearby grocery stores
Transportation barriers
Economic constraints
Limited awareness of available food resources

Small neighborhood stores often stock mostly processed or shelf-stable foods, which contributes to poor nutrition outcomes.

This project explores how technology and geographic visualization can help communities discover and access nearby food resources.

# Solution: 

Meals-on-Wheels provides an interactive web platform that allows users to:

Discover nearby food access points
Visualize food distribution coverage areas
Explore meal options
Improve awareness of local nutrition resources

By combining mapping technology with community food data, the platform helps residents quickly find accessible food resources.
