# Pricing and subsidy notes

This purchase-focused prototype uses the uploaded USDA Low-Cost Food Plan PDF as the nutrition-basket basis for subsidized boxes.

The PDF explains that USDA food plans provide representative healthful market baskets at different cost levels. It also describes the Low-Cost Food Plan as a lower-cost nutritious market basket and shows the basket structure across grains, vegetables, fruits, milk products, and meat/beans.

## How the website applies that

The subsidized boxes are not single discounted items. Each box combines:
- Grain or bread/pantry base
- Vegetable and/or fruit
- Protein source
- Healthy snack or dairy item where appropriate

## Prototype subsidized prices

- Campus Dorm Box: $8.49, 6 meal/snack portions, about $1.42 per portion.
- Solo Low-Cost Box: $6.49, 4 portions, about $1.62 per portion.
- Vegetarian Support Box: $7.49, 5 portions, about $1.50 per portion.
- Family Community Box: $15.99, 10 portions, about $1.60 per portion.

These are hackathon target prices. Actual launch prices should be finalized with wholesale costs, subsidy dollars, SNAP/EBT rules, store commission, spoilage, labor, cold storage, delivery costs, and live DC-area retailer comparisons.
